Angle Information

Common Angle References:
  • Full Circle: 360° (2π rad, 400 grad)
  • Half Circle: 180° (π rad, 200 grad)
  • Quarter Circle: 90° (π/2 rad, 100 grad)
  • Right Angle: 90° (π/2 rad)
  • Straight Angle: 180° (π rad)
  • Acute Angle: < 90° (< π/2 rad)
  • Obtuse Angle: 90°-180° (π/2-π rad)
  • Reflex Angle: 180°-360° (π-2π rad)
Angle Units Explained:
  • Degrees: Most common unit (360° = full circle)
  • Radians: SI unit (2π rad = full circle)
  • Gradians: Metric unit (400 grad = full circle)
  • Arcminutes: 1/60 of a degree (60' = 1°)
  • Arcseconds: 1/60 of arcminute (3600" = 1°)
  • Turns: Complete rotations (1 turn = 360°)
  • Quadrants: Quarter circles (4 quadrants = full circle)
  • Binary Degrees: 256 binary degrees = full circle

Angle Applications

Mathematical Applications:
  • Trigonometry: Sine, cosine, tangent functions
  • Geometry: Polygon angles, circle measurements
  • Calculus: Derivatives, integrals with angles
  • Physics: Rotational motion, wave functions
  • Engineering: Structural design, mechanical systems
  • Navigation: Compass bearings, GPS coordinates
  • Astronomy: Celestial coordinates, star positions
  • Computer Graphics: 3D rotations, animations
Practical Uses:
  • Construction: Building angles, roof pitches
  • Surveying: Land measurements, property boundaries
  • Machining: Tool angles, cutting operations
  • Photography: Camera angles, lens field of view
  • Sports: Golf swings, basketball shots
  • Art: Perspective drawing, composition
  • Medicine: Joint angles, range of motion
  • Robotics: Joint rotations, arm movements
