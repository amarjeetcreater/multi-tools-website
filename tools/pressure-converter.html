Pressure Information

Common Pressure References:
  • Atmospheric Pressure: 101,325 Pa (1 atm)
  • Car Tire: 30-35 PSI (207-241 kPa)
  • Bicycle Tire: 60-120 PSI (414-828 kPa)
  • Scuba Tank: 3,000 PSI (20.7 MPa)
  • Hydraulic System: 2,000-4,000 PSI (14-28 MPa)
  • Blood Pressure: 120/80 mmHg
  • Vacuum Cleaner: 20-30 inHg
  • Deep Ocean: 1,000+ atm
Pressure Units Explained:
  • Pascal: SI unit (1 Pa = 1 N/m²)
  • Bar: Metric unit (1 bar = 100,000 Pa)
  • PSI: Imperial unit (1 PSI = 6,895 Pa)
  • Atmosphere: Standard atmospheric pressure
  • Torr: Based on mercury column (1 torr = 133.3 Pa)
  • mmHg: Millimeters of mercury
  • kgf/cm²: Kilogram-force per square centimeter
  • Dyne/cm²: CGS unit (1 dyn/cm² = 0.1 Pa)

Pressure Types

Types of Pressure:
  • Absolute Pressure: Total pressure including atmospheric
  • Gauge Pressure: Pressure relative to atmospheric
  • Differential Pressure: Difference between two pressures
  • Vacuum Pressure: Pressure below atmospheric
  • Hydrostatic Pressure: Pressure due to fluid weight
  • Dynamic Pressure: Pressure due to fluid motion
  • Static Pressure: Pressure in stationary fluid
  • Stagnation Pressure: Total pressure in moving fluid
Pressure Applications:
  • Weather: Barometric pressure for forecasting
  • Automotive: Tire pressure, engine oil pressure
  • Medical: Blood pressure, respiratory pressure
  • Industrial: Hydraulic systems, pneumatic tools
  • Aviation: Cabin pressure, altitude measurement
  • Diving: Water pressure at different depths
  • Manufacturing: Process pressure, quality control
  • Research: Laboratory pressure measurements
