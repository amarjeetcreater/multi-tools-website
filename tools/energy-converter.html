Energy Information

Common Energy References:
  • Apple: 80 calories (335 J)
  • Light Bulb (1 hour): 60 Wh (216,000 J)
  • Car Battery: 50 kWh (180 MJ)
  • Household (daily): 30 kWh (108 MJ)
  • Nuclear Bomb: 15 kilotons (63 TJ)
  • Sun (per second): 3.8×10²⁶ W
  • Human Daily: 2,000 kcal (8.4 MJ)
  • Lightning Bolt: 1 billion J (1 GJ)
Energy Units Explained:
  • Joule: SI unit of energy (1 J = 1 N⋅m)
  • Calorie: Energy to heat 1g water by 1°C
  • Kilowatt-hour: Energy used by 1kW for 1 hour
  • Electron Volt: Energy gained by electron in 1V
  • BTU: British thermal unit (heat energy)
  • Foot-pound: Work done by 1 lb force over 1 ft
  • Erg: CGS unit (1 erg = 10⁻⁷ J)
  • Therm: 100,000 BTU (natural gas)

Energy Types

Forms of Energy:
  • Kinetic: Energy of motion
  • Potential: Stored energy (gravity, elastic)
  • Thermal: Heat energy
  • Electrical: Energy from electric charges
  • Chemical: Energy stored in bonds
  • Nuclear: Energy from atomic nuclei
  • Radiant: Light and electromagnetic energy
  • Sound: Energy of vibrations
Energy Conservation:
  • Law of Conservation: Energy cannot be created or destroyed
  • Energy Transfer: Energy moves between systems
  • Efficiency: Useful energy output / total input
  • Renewable: Solar, wind, hydro, geothermal
  • Non-renewable: Fossil fuels, nuclear
  • Energy Density: Energy per unit mass
  • Power: Rate of energy transfer (watts)
  • Work: Energy transferred by force
