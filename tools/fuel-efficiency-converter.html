Fuel Efficiency Information

Common Efficiency References:
  • Hybrid Car: 50-60 MPG (4.7-5.6 L/100km)
  • Electric Car: 100+ MPGe (equivalent)
  • Compact Car: 30-40 MPG (5.9-7.8 L/100km)
  • SUV: 20-30 MPG (7.8-11.8 L/100km)
  • Truck: 15-25 MPG (9.4-15.7 L/100km)
  • Motorcycle: 40-60 MPG (3.9-5.9 L/100km)
  • Diesel Car: 35-45 MPG (5.2-6.7 L/100km)
  • Sports Car: 15-25 MPG (9.4-15.7 L/100km)
Efficiency Units Explained:
  • MPG: Miles per gallon (distance per fuel)
  • L/100km: Liters per 100 kilometers (fuel per distance)
  • km/L: Kilometers per liter (distance per fuel)
  • US vs UK: Different gallon sizes (3.785L vs 4.546L)
  • Imperial MPG: UK gallons with imperial miles
  • gal/100mi: Gallons per 100 miles
  • L/km: Liters per kilometer
  • km/gal: Kilometers per gallon

Fuel Efficiency Tips

Driving Tips:
  • Maintain Speed: Avoid rapid acceleration and braking
  • Proper Tire Pressure: Check regularly for optimal efficiency
  • Remove Weight: Remove unnecessary items from vehicle
  • Use Cruise Control: Maintain consistent speed on highways
  • Avoid Idling: Turn off engine when stopped for long periods
  • Plan Routes: Avoid traffic and choose efficient paths
  • Regular Maintenance: Keep engine and filters clean
  • Use AC Wisely: AC reduces efficiency by 10-20%
Vehicle Factors:
  • Engine Size: Smaller engines generally more efficient
  • Aerodynamics: Streamlined design reduces drag
  • Weight: Lighter vehicles use less fuel
  • Transmission: Automatic vs manual affects efficiency
  • Fuel Type: Different fuels have different energy content
  • Age: Newer vehicles often more efficient
  • Technology: Hybrid and electric options available
  • Driving Conditions: City vs highway affects efficiency
